JAMHacks 4 is the largest high school hackathon in Waterloo!

What is JAMHacks?

JAMHacks is a hackathon—a 24-hour-long “innovation marathon.” You’ll work in teams of up to 4 to create an app, game, website, robot, gadget, or whatever your heart desires.

What else is JAMHacks?

JAMHacks is also a learnathon—There will be workshops and mentors to guide you through making a software or electronics project, even if you’ve never coded before!

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don’t know how to code?

JAMHacks is a hackathon—a 24-hour-long “innovation marathon.” You’ll work in teams of up to 4 to create an app, game, website, robot, gadget, or whatever your heart desires.

Do I need to have a team?

Nope! We’ll have time for team formation at the event, which is a great way to meet cool new people. That said, if you have some friends, you can definitely form a team ahead of time!

Can I build a hardware/electronics project?

Absolutely! We’ll have a shop open with soldering irons and small components (resistors etc.) for you to use.

Who can participate?

JAMHacks is open to any high school student in Ontario! You don’t need to have any experience! We will be accepting everyone who registers!

Is there any cost?

Nope! The event will be free for all participants thanks to the generous contributions of our sponsors.

What should I bring?

Bring your laptop, phone, charger, a reusable water bottle, sleeping bag, toiletries, and your student card or some form of ID. We will be providing meals and snacks, and sleeping rooms!

What if I’m not a high school student?

We’d love to have you as a mentor! You can register here.

Is there a code of conduct?

Is there a code of conduct?Yes. In short, be respectful to other people. You can read the full version here.

More questions? Feel free to email us at hello@jamhacks.ca
